Udaipur to Nathdwara by cab: Shrinathji darshan in a day

By Aavre    July 2026    6 min read

For a lot of visitors to Udaipur, a morning at Nathdwara is the spiritual heart of the trip. The town is built around the Shrinathji temple, one of the most revered Krishna shrines in India, and the darshan here has a warmth and intimacy that stays with people long after they leave.

The best part is how easy it is. Nathdwara is close, the road is good, and you can be there and back comfortably in a day, or even a half day if you plan it well. Here is how.

How far is Nathdwara from Udaipur?

Nathdwara sits about 48 kilometres north of Udaipur, in the Rajsamand district on the banks of the Banas river. The drive takes roughly an hour each way on NH 58, a smooth and straightforward highway run. That closeness is what makes Nathdwara such a popular trip. You can leave after breakfast, take your darshan and be back in Udaipur by the afternoon.

Because it is so quick, many people pair Nathdwara with a stop or two along the way rather than treating it as a full day on its own. More on that below.

The drive itself

The road out of Udaipur towards Nathdwara runs north through the Aravalli foothills and open countryside. It is one of the gentler drives in the region, mostly highway, passing small towns and roadside stalls selling buttermilk, jaggery and seasonal produce. On the way you pass close to Eklingji, the ancient Shiva temple of the Mewar royal family, which many travellers fold into the same trip.

It is an easy drive in a comfortable car, and with light traffic you are in the temple town in about an hour.

The Shrinathji temple follows a fixed schedule of darshan slots through the day, and the deity is treated as a living presence, with times set aside for waking, meals and rest. Photography and phones are not allowed inside the temple, and there are cloakrooms outside for your belongings. Check the day's darshan timings before you leave and aim to arrive a little early, especially on weekends and festival days.

The Shrinathji temple

Shrinathji is a form of Lord Krishna as a seven year old child, and the temple at Nathdwara is the most important shrine of the Pushtimarg tradition founded by Vallabhacharya. The word Nathdwara itself means the gateway to Shrinathji. The deity was brought here in the seventeenth century and a temple was built under the protection of the Mewar rulers, and it has been a centre of devotion ever since.

What makes the darshan special is the sense of the deity as a living being. Through the day Shrinathji is bathed, dressed, offered food and put to rest, and each darshan slot shows a different moment in that daily routine. It is unlike most temple visits, and it is worth understanding a little of the rhythm before you go so the experience makes sense.

More than the temple

Nathdwara is also famous for Pichwai painting, the intricate cloth art that depicts scenes from Krishna's life and is traditionally hung behind the deity. The lanes around the temple are full of workshops and shops where you can watch artists at work and buy a piece to take home. If you have time after darshan, it is a lovely and unhurried thing to do.

Combining Nathdwara with nearby stops

Because the trip is short, Nathdwara pairs naturally with other sights in the same direction. Eklingji, the Shiva temple complex, sits on the way back towards Udaipur. Haldighati, the historic battlefield where Maharana Pratap fought the Mughals, is a short drive from Nathdwara and has a museum worth an hour. Some travellers even continue on to Kumbhalgarh or Ranakpur, which lie further along the same road, though that turns it into a longer, fuller day.

Tell your driver what you would like to see when you book, and the day can be planned around the darshan timings and the stops you want.

How much time you need

The darshan itself and a wander through the Pichwai shops can be done in two to three hours. Add the hour of driving at each end and Nathdwara sits comfortably in a half day, leaving your afternoon free back in Udaipur. If you are adding Eklingji or Haldighati, plan for most of the day.

Best time to visit

October through March is the most comfortable season, with cool, pleasant weather for the drive and the walk to the temple. Festival days, especially Janmashtami, Holi and Diwali, are extraordinary at Nathdwara but extremely crowded, so plan carefully if you want to visit around them. Summers are hot, so an early start is your friend if you are travelling between April and June.
